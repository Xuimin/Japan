ABOUT JAPAN

Tokyo

Tokyo is Japan's capital and largest city located at the head of Tokyo Bay. It mixes the traditional and modern from skyscrapers and museums. As the most modern, most varied city in Japan, Tokyo offers a mix of old and new like nowhere else.

Osaka

Osaka is Japan's second largest and it is a great place to experience a large, modern city known for their nightlife, street foods and Osaka Castle.

Hiroshima

Hiroshima is located in the southwest of Japan. It has a rich topography with the islands and the clear water. When the first atomic bomb was dropped over Hiroshima, the city became known worldwide for this unenviable distinction.

Kobe

Kobe is the seventh-largest city in Japan and the capital city of Hyōgo Prefecture. It is known as a port city, Kobe's picture-perfect location between the sea and the Rokko mountains is its best feature.

Kyoto

Kyoto is Japan’s ancient tradition. It is famous for its Buddhist temples, gardens and traditional wooden house. Kyoto never stops fascinating visitors with the extraordinary townscape with beautiful shrines and temples. It used to be an ancient capital of Japan until Tokyo took over.

Japan is an island country in East Asia and is located in the Northwest Pacific Ocean. Japan is the eleventh-most populous country in the world. In Japanese, the name “Japan” is Nihon or Nippon, which means “Land of the Rising Sun.” It was once believed that Japan was the first country to see the sun rise in the East in the morning.

JAPANESE FOODS

Japanese food is one of the most popular cuisines in the world and for good reasons.
Based on “rules of five,” traditional Japanese cooking emphasizes variety and balance.

SUSHI

Sushi is prepared vinegared rice, usually with some sugar and salt, accompanying a variety of ingredients such as seafood, often raw, and vegetables.

UDON

Udon is a thick noodle made from wheat flour. It is a comfort food for many Japanese people.

TEMPURA

Tempura is a typical Japanese dish usually consisting of seafood, meat, and vegetables that have been battered and deep fried.

ONIGIRI

Onigiri is made from white rice formed into triangular or cylindrical shapes and often wrapped in nori(seaweed).

RAMEN

Ramen is basically known as "pulled noodles" is a Japanese noodle soup. It consists of Chinese-style wheat noodles.

DONBURI

Donburi is known as "rice-bowl dish" consisting of fish, meat, vegetables or other ingredients simmered together and served over rice.

TAMAGOYAKI

Tamagoyaki is a type of Japanese omelet, which is made by rolling together several layers of fried egg.

MOCHI

Mochi is Japanese rice cake made of mochigome, a short-grain japonica glutinous rice, and sometimes other ingredients. The rice is pounded into paste and molded into the desired shape.

UNAGI

Unagi is a hearty, nutritious meal loaded with vitamins and protein that's very popular in Japan. Usually served with a sweet soy teriyaki sauce over rice, the Japanese believe that eating eel during the hottest months helps fight fatigue.

MISO SOUP

Miso Soup is made from a combination of miso paste. Tofu and seaweed are usually added in it.

OKONOMIYAKI

Okonomiyaki is savory pancake made by mixing together batter and sliced cabbage.

CHAWANMUSHI

Chawanmushi is savory egg custard steamed in a tea bowl.

ATTRACTIONS

There are many attractions to visit and places to see in Japan. Here are some...

Hiroshima Peace Memorial

Hiroshima Peace Memorial is created in April 1, 1954 in Hiroshima. Originally, it is known as Hiroshima Prefectural Commercial Exhibition (HMI) for the purpose of arts and educational exhibition. After the atomic bomb was dropped in World War II, it is the only building left standing. Hence, it changed its name to Hiroshima Peace Memorial. The museum takes us back to World War II and reminds people that peace is what we need in this world. It also reminds people of the importance of human life as well as honors the victim so they will not be forgotten.

Himeji Castle

The Himeji Castle is a hilltop Japanese castle complex situated in the city of Himeji which is located in the Hyōgo Prefecture of Japan. It is considered the best existing example of Japanese castle architecture. It was fortified to defend against enemies during the feudal period, but it has been rebuilt many times throughout the centuries and reflects the different design periods. It survived the bombings of World War II and is frequently seen in domestic and foreign films, including the James Bond movie “You Only Live Twice”.

Tokyo Tower

The Tokyo Tower also known as “Japan Radio Tower” is a testament to the advancement of technology and modern life. It is inspired by the Eiffel tower design and is the second tallest man-made structure in Japan. It functions as a communications and observation tower. Visitors can climb the tower for unparalleled views of Tokyo and the surrounding areas as well as visit shops and restaurants.

Tokyo Imperial Palace

Tokyo Imperial Palace is located in the center of Tokyo in the Chiyoda district. It stands out as a unique ancient architecture among the urban skyscrapers. The Palace is divided into the eastern part and the western part by the Kan-Moat and Hasuike Moat. The eastern part is called the Imperial Palace Eastern Garden and has been open to the public since 1968. In the western part are: the Fukiage Gyoen (Garden).

Mount Fuji

Mount Fuji is the highest mountain in Japan at 3,776 meters. It is the second-highest volcano located on an island in Asia (after Mount Kerinci on the island of Sumatra), and seventh-highest peak of an island on Earth. The volcano’s exceptionally symmetrical cone is a well-known symbol of Japan and it is frequently depicted in art and photographs, as well as a popular tourist attraction for sightseers and climbers.

Culture

Here are some Japan's culture.

Onsen

Onsen are the country's hot springs bathing facilities and traditional inns around them. As a volcanically active country, Japan has thousands of onsens scattered throughout all of its major islands.

Origami

Origami is originated in Japan. It is the art of folding paper, is often associated with Japanese culture. Origami aims to fold a plain sheet of paper into beautiful as well as interesting sculptures and intricate designs. Traditionally, origami has been practiced in Japan since the Edo period in the early 17th century.

National flower: Sakura (Cherry Blossoms)
National garment: Kimono

The kimono is a T-shaped, wrapped-front garment with square sleeves and a rectangular body, and is worn left side wrapped over right.

INTERESTING FACTS

Here are some you might not know about Japan.

Square Watermelon

It was invented back in the ‘70s by a graphic designer for the purpose of easier to cut, stack as well as store. It is grown in containers. Since it is pretty expensive, people don’t buy them as food but rather as a decorative item.

Slurp Noodles

It is appropriate to slurp noodles. It’s a way to give your compliments to the chef. In fact, you’re far more likely to be seen as rude if you don’t slurp your noodles, as it suggests that you’re not enjoying the food.

Bowing

When Japanese people meet, they bow instead of shaking hands. Lowest bow shows deepest respect.

No four

Japan avoid the number four (shi) because it sounds the same as the word death. Tall building does not have fourth floor. Some event even cannot have four people only.

Vending Machines

There are over 5 million vending machines with one on almost every street corner. You can buy almost anything you want here.

Did you know?

Japan is substantially prone to earthquakes, tsunami and volcanoes because of its location along the “Pacific Ring of Fire”. Japan has 111 active volcanoes. Destructive earthquakes, often resulting in tsunami, this occurs several times each century.
